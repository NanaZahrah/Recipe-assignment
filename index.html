JOLLOF RICE WITH PEPPERED TURKEY

This recipe features smoky, flavorful Jollof rice served with spicy, well-seasoned peppered turkey. Jollof rice is a classic West African dish loved for its rich flavor and smoky aroma. Paired with peppered turkey, it becomes the perfect meal for celebrations, family gatherings, or just a hearty weekend lunch.

RECIPE INFORMATION

  • Preparation time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking time: 1 hour
  • Servings: 5 people
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

INGREDIENTS

FOR JOLLOF RICE

  • 3 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 6 large tomatoes
  • 4 red bell peppers (Tatashe)
  • 1 large and 1 medium sized onion
  • 10 scotch bonnet peppers (Atarodo)
  • 1/2 cup vegetable or sunflower oil
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 teaspoons of curry powder
  • 2 teaspoons of thyme
  • 2 cups of chicken stock
  • 3 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste

FOR THE PEPPERED TURKEY

  • 1.5kg turkey wings or drumsticks
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers
  • 1 large onion
  • 1 teaspoon ginger powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil

INSTRUCTIONS

FOR COOKING THE JOLLOF RICE

  1. Rinse and parboil the rice for about 10 minutes, drain and set aside
  2. Blend the tomatoes, bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and 1 large onion until smooth.
  3. Slice the medium sized onion into rings
  4. Heat oil in a pot, fry the sliced onions for 2 minutes, then add tomato paste and fry for another 2 minutes.
  5. Add the blended pepper mix, season with curry powder, thyme, seasoning cubes, and salt. Stir well and allow to cook until the oil begins to float on top.
  6. Add chicken stock and bring to a boil, then pour in the parboiled rice.
  7. Cover with foil and a tight-fittimg lid, reduce the heat, and steam for 25-30 minutes
  8. Stir gently, adjust seasoning, add cook for another 5 mintes. Set aside.

FOR COOKING THE PEPPERED TURKEY

  1. Season turkey with ginger powder, garlic powder, paprika, thyme, seasoning cubes, and salt. Leave to marinate for a few minutes then boil until tender.
  2. Blend the bel peppers, scotch bonnets, and onion
  3. Heat oil in a pan, pour the pepper mix, season to your taste and cook until thick and flavorful
  4. Add the boiled turkey pieces to the pepper sauce and stir-fry for about 5-7 minutes untilwell coated.
  5. Serve hot with the jollof rice.

TIPS

For extra smoky flavor in your jollof, reduce water and let the rice steam slowly. You can allow to burn slightly to the smoky bottom-pot jollof taste. For the turkey, grill slightly before adding to the pepper sauce for a delicious charred taste.
